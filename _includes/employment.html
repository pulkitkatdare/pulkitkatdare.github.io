Co-ordination Control for a Swarm of Purcell's Swimmers

Masters thesis, @IIT Bombay, India
2017 to Present

Micro-swimming is an exciting new field of science wherein the primary focus of researchers has been to understand the mechanisms exhibited by different kind of microorganisms to aid in it’s swimming. The interest of the engineering community in studying this kind of physics is to possibly emulate this in fabricating micro-robotic swimmers also popularly called as micro-swimmers to realise many potential engineering and medicinal applications. One of the future envisioned applications of micro-swimmers is to aid in detecting and eliminating cancerous cells in the human body. To realise such a potential application may require us to deploy one or more such micro-swimmers in the human body navigating in co-ordination with each other. This co-ordination problem requires us to not only have a thorough understanding about some fundamental concepts of microswimming but also about this immense field of co-ordination control. In this report we attempt to understand the various aspects of co-ordination or formation control, we later try to emulate this co-ordination theories to solve a formation control problem involving a bunch of Purcell’s Micro-swimmers.(Link)

Modelling and Control of Shape Altering Micro-Swimmer

Bachelor's thesis, @IIT Bombay, India
2016 to 2017

In this BTP work we propose a model for a micro-swimmer which moves using internal shape changes of its surface to aid in its locomotion. We later go on to propose a control-affine model for this micro-swimmer which exists in SE(2) space.But in order to answer the question as to whether it is possible for this swimmer to reach each and every point in this SE(2) space is later proved by our geometric controllability result. We finally conclude our work with proposing motion primitive controls for our swimmer.(here)

Trajectory tracking using motion primitives for the Purcell’s swimmer

@IIT Bombay, India
December 2016 to February 2016

This work involved around implementing the motion primitive controls proposed for a Purcells swimmer. To implement this we fabricated a macro model for the Purcells swimmer which was 3D printed. We initially implemented motion primitive algorithm to move the swimmer in basis direction i.e x,y,θ. The algorithm was later extended to track a certain predefined trajectory using an open loop control system. This work of ours was submitted to IROS 2017 conference and selected for an oral presentation. (here)

Dust Characterization of Keyboard Connector

Research Scholar, @University of Maryland, USA
May 2016 to July 2016

This work was a part of a 10-week internship as a visiting scholar at, CALCE lab University of Maryland. CALCE lab in one of the few leading labs researching in life cycle engineering at the University of Maryland. This work involved investigating failure of certain keyboard connectors which were suspiciously reported to CALCE about its inability to work. This work involved testing of keyboard connector to check its resistivity to confirm whether there has been a short circuit. Finally we tested the dust particles actually present on the keyboard and scanning them on SEM-EDX to check its size and their composition.
